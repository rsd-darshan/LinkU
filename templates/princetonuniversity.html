Princeton University Financial Aid Principles

When awarding aid, we are guided by the following principles:

  • Admission is need-blind for all applicants, including international students. Applying for aid does not disadvantage applicants in the admission process, ensuring equality of opportunity for low- and middle-income students.
  • Princeton financial aid is awarded solely based on need; there are no merit scholarships. Academic and athletic achievements are considered only in relation to admission.
  • Need is determined through a careful review of each family's individual financial circumstances. Personal attention is given to every admitted student's aid application, accounting for special circumstances and professional judgment.
  • The full demonstrated need of every admitted student is met through grants, not loans.

What Princeton Looks for in Applicants

  • 📚Intellectual Curiosity: Princeton seeks students who are intellectually curious and have demonstrated academic excellence.
  • 🏅Personal and Extracurricular Accomplishments: The university values applicants with strong personal achievements and who have excelled in extracurricular activities.
  • 💬Authentic Storytelling: Princeton wants students who can share their unique stories and highlight what makes them special, both academically and personally.
  • 🌍Community Engagement: The university appreciates students who actively contribute to their communities and are committed to making a positive difference.
  • 🎓Future Contributions: Princeton looks for students who will make a lasting impact on campus and beyond, showcasing values like responsibility, diversity, inclusion, and drive.

Suggestions to Get Into Princeton

  • 📖Strong Academic Preparation: Complete a rigorous course load, including classes in English, math, foreign language, lab sciences, and history, and take advantage of AP, IB, or honors courses where available.
  • 📝Craft Authentic Essays: Write essays that genuinely reflect your voice, personality, and values. Avoid external help that compromises intellectual integrity.
  • 🌟Highlight Extracurricular Impact: Showcase your contributions to your community, leadership activities, and other engagements that demonstrate your commitment and growth.
  • 🔍Reflect on Personal Values: Think about what drives you, the values you uphold, and the ways you've made an impact in your communities. Princeton values students who bring their full selves to campus.
  • 📜Showcase Integrity: Ensure that your application materials are your own work and reflect your unique experiences, ambitions, and contributions.
  • 🔗Complete All Application Materials: Refer to the First-Year Application Checklist to ensure all materials, such as transcripts, recommendations, and the Princeton-specific questions, are completed and submitted.

Princeton's Graded Written Paper

Princeton requires a high school graded paper that is expository, 1-2 pages, and preferably from an English, history, or social studies class. It should include teacher comments and a rubric, but quality is valued over the grade itself.

Non-traditional applicants have specific guidelines for submitting papers. For more, refer to Princeton’s admissions page.

Tips for an Effective Princeton Graded Paper

  • Choose Expository Writing: Select an essay that presents analysis or arguments rather than creative writing. English and history essays are ideal.
  • Highlight Teacher Feedback: Papers with teacher comments provide valuable insights. Include any rubric if possible.
  • Focus on Quality, Not Grades: Choose a paper that represents your best writing, even if it’s not your highest grade.
  • Keep It Concise: Aim for 1-2 pages, but a strong paper up to 3-5 pages is acceptable.

Princeton's Supplemental Essay Prompts

Prompt #1: For A.B. Degree Applicants or Those Who Are Undecided

As a research institution that also prides itself on its liberal arts curriculum, Princeton allows students to explore areas across the humanities and the arts, the natural sciences, and the social sciences. What academic areas most pique your curiosity, and how do the programs offered at Princeton suit your particular interests? (Please respond in 250 words or fewer.)

Prompt #2: For B.S.E Degree Applicants

Please describe why you are interested in studying engineering at Princeton. Include any of your experiences in or exposure to engineering, and how you think the programs offered at the University suit your particular interests. (Please respond in 250 words or fewer.)

Prompt #3: Your Voice

Princeton values community and encourages students, faculty, staff, and leadership to engage in respectful conversations that can expand their perspectives and challenge their ideas and beliefs. As a prospective member of this community, reflect on how your lived experiences will impact the conversations you will have in the classroom, the dining hall, or other campus spaces. What lessons have you learned in life thus far? What will your classmates learn from you? In short, how has your lived experience shaped you? (Please respond in 500 words or fewer.)

Prompt #4: Civic Engagement

Princeton has a longstanding commitment to understanding our responsibility to society through service and civic engagement. How does your own story intersect with these ideals? (Please respond in 250 words or fewer.)

Prompt #5: More About You

Please respond to each question in 50 words or fewer. There are no right or wrong answers. Be yourself!
