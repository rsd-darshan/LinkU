Duke University Financial Aid Principles

When awarding undergraduate financial aid, Duke adheres to the following guiding principles:

  • Parents and students have the primary responsibility to contribute to educational expenses, based on their financial ability, before Duke awards financial aid.
  • Families with similar financial profiles are expected to contribute similar amounts. Both income and assets, including business assets, are evaluated to determine financial need.
  • Institutional aid is awarded solely on financial need, as determined by the information provided in an applicant’s CSS Profile and supporting documentation.
  • Merit-based scholarships are separate from financial aid, with most merit scholarships automatically considered through the Duke admissions process.
  • Financial aid includes a combination of grants, loans, and work-study. Aid is designed to ensure affordability for students from diverse financial backgrounds.
  • If your family's total income is above $40,000, part of the aid offered will include a federal loan. Federal student loans are considered the best loan option for students.
  • Loans are capped at a maximum of $5,000 per year in your aid offer, ensuring manageable debt upon graduation.
  • Work-study is included in financial aid packages, with students typically having $2,200 allocated for work-study. Eligibility for Federal Work Study is based on FAFSA and federal need; otherwise, Duke Work Study is offered.
  • After loans and work-study are determined, the remaining financial aid is provided as grant aid. These grants may come from Duke's endowments or other resources.

How Your Expected Contribution Is Determined

Duke calculates an Expected Family Contribution (EFC) for all students who submit the required financial documents, including the FAFSA, CSS Profile, and requested tax forms. The contribution is based on:

  • Income: Two years prior to the student's enrollment (e.g., tax year 2022 for 2024-25 applicants).
  • Allowances: Includes deductions for federal and state taxes, medical expenses, education savings, and private school tuition for siblings.
  • Basic Living Expenses: Adjusted for the number of family members and regional cost of living.

In addition, students are expected to contribute to their educational costs as follows:

Duke University Scholarships

While most financial aid is need-based, Duke offers several merit scholarships, including:

  • Karsh International Scholarship: For international students with outstanding academic and global impact potential.
  • Angier B. Duke Scholarship: For students demonstrating intellectual accomplishment and potential for academic leadership.
  • Benjamin N. Duke Scholarship: For North and South Carolina students excelling academically and in community engagement.
  • David M. Rubenstein Scholarship: For first-generation college students.
  • Trinity Scholarships: For students with exceptional academic performance from North and South Carolina.
  • University Scholarship: For students passionate about interdisciplinary research and collaborative innovation.

Merit Scholars' Opportunities

Merit Scholars at Duke benefit from:

  • Faculty Engagement: Faculty Directors and mentors guide scholars through programs, including research, guest lectures, and academic conversations.
  • Enrichment Experiences: Scholars can use enrichment funds for research, internships, service projects, or creative endeavors.
  • Professional Development: Opportunities for mentoring and securing research positions begin in the first year.

What Duke Looks for in Applicants

Duke University seeks students who are ready to engage intelligently, creatively, and enthusiastically with the multitude of opportunities the university offers. Admissions officers look for ambition, curiosity, talent, persistence, energy, and humanity. The goal is to build a dynamic and collaborative community of intellectual explorers who will make an impact at Duke and beyond.

Key Qualities Duke Looks For

  • 📚Academic Excellence: Duke emphasizes a rigorous academic program. Students are encouraged to challenge themselves with the most demanding courses available. Academic performance, as reflected in grades, is one of the most important factors in the application process.
  • 💬Teacher and Counselor Recommendations: Recommendations provide insights into a student’s intellectual curiosity, classroom behavior, relationships, and overall potential.
  • 🎓Extracurricular Involvement: Duke values students with a genuine passion for extracurricular activities, whether they demonstrate leadership, creativity, or commitment to community service.
  • 📝Personal Essays: The application essays offer students a chance to showcase their unique voice, share personal experiences, and express their intellectual and personal interests.
  • 🌍Character and Resilience: Duke seeks students who have overcome challenges, demonstrated resilience, and shown a commitment to growth.

Testing and Academic Standards

Duke evaluates standardized test scores as part of a holistic review. The middle 50% range for admitted students in the Class of 2027 was:

  • SAT Composite: 1520-1570
  • ACT Composite: 33-35

While Duke is test-optional, applicants who choose to submit scores should understand they are evaluated in the context of the entire application.

What Sets Duke Apart

Duke seeks students who will contribute meaningfully to the university's vibrant campus culture. Whether through athletics, research, service, or other activities, students should demonstrate a willingness to lead and collaborate. The admissions process evaluates each applicant’s story and potential to thrive at Duke.

Tips for Duke Applicants

  • Take Rigorous Courses: Enroll in five academic courses per year, ensuring you challenge yourself across subject areas.
  • Focus on Essays: Duke values authentic essays that provide insight into your personality and interests. Carefully craft both your long and short responses.
  • Choose Recommenders Thoughtfully: Select teachers and a counselor who know you well and can highlight your strengths and contributions.
  • Show Demonstrated Interest: Attend admissions events, connect with Duke representatives, and engage with the university through visits or virtual interactions.

Duke’s admissions process aims to identify students who excel academically, are passionate about their pursuits, and will bring diverse perspectives to the university community. The goal is to admit students who will make the most of Duke’s resources and leave a positive impact on campus and the world.

Duke's Supplemental Essay Prompts

Prompt #1: "Why Duke?"

What is your sense of Duke as a university and a community, and why do you consider it a good match for you? If there's something in particular about our offerings that attracts you, feel free to share that as well. (250 word limit)

Prompts #2 and #3: Optional Questions

We want to emphasize that the following questions are optional. Feel free to answer them if you believe that doing so will add something meaningful that is not already shared elsewhere in your application. A maximum of two questions can be selected. (250 word limit for each)

  • Perspective Response: We believe a wide range of personal perspectives, beliefs, and lived experiences are essential to making Duke a vibrant and meaningful living and learning community. Feel free to share with us anything in this context that might help us better understand you and what you might bring to our community.
  • Intellectual Experience: Tell us about an experience in the past year or two that reflects your imagination, creativity, or intellect.
  • Beliefs & Values: We believe there is benefit in sharing or questioning our beliefs or values; who do you agree with on the big important things, or who do you have your most interesting disagreements with? What are you agreeing or disagreeing about?
  • Orientation, Identity, Expression: Duke’s commitment to inclusion and belonging includes sexual orientation, gender identity, and gender expression. Feel free to share with us more about how your identity in this context has meaning for you as an individual or as a member of a community.
  • Being Different: We recognize that not fully “fitting in” a community or place can sometimes be difficult. Duke values the effort, resilience, and independence that may require. Feel free to share with us circumstances where something about you is different and how that has influenced your experiences or identity.
