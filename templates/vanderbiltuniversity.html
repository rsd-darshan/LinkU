Vanderbilt University Financial Aid Principles

At Vanderbilt, we believe cost should never be a barrier to a world-class education. That‚Äôs why we provide a full-tuition scholarship for all households with income of $150K* or less. Many households with income above $150K may still qualify for an Opportunity Vanderbilt Award.

Opportunity Vanderbilt reflects our belief that a world-renowned education should be accessible to all qualified students, regardless of economic circumstances. To ensure this, Vanderbilt has made the following commitments to U.S. Citizens and eligible non-citizens:

  • Admission is need-blind for U.S. Citizens and eligible non-citizens. Applying for financial aid does not affect admission decisions, ensuring access to students from all backgrounds.
  • Vanderbilt meets 100% of a family‚Äôs demonstrated financial need.
  • Financial aid awards include grants and scholarships, without loans, making Vanderbilt among a select few institutions adopting these philosophies.

Vanderbilt University Financial Aid Principles for International Students

At Vanderbilt, we believe that cost should not be a barrier to a world-class education, and we are committed to making financial aid available to international students. While U.S. citizens and eligible non-citizens benefit from need-blind admission, Vanderbilt offers need-based aid to a limited number of international students.

For international students, Vanderbilt is dedicated to providing the following principles regarding financial aid:

  • International students seeking need-based aid will be considered on a need-aware basis, with decisions based on academic excellence, leadership, community involvement, and financial need.
  • Vanderbilt offers need-based scholarships that help meet a portion of a family‚Äôs demonstrated financial need, though availability of resources may limit the number of awards.
  • Vanderbilt provides generous merit-based scholarships for international students, recognizing exceptional talent and achievements in various fields.
  • Financial aid packages for international students may include a mix of need-based aid and merit-based scholarships, and all awards are designed to reduce the burden of tuition costs.

  • Take Challenging Courses: Ensure you are enrolled in the most rigorous courses available, including AP, IB, or dual-enrollment classes if offered. Vanderbilt values academic rigor in your high school curriculum.
  • Write Thoughtful Essays: Use the personal essays to showcase your unique voice, experiences, and intellectual curiosity. Reflect on how you embody Vanderbilt‚Äôs values and what you will bring to the campus community.
  • Choose Recommenders Wisely: Select teachers and a counselor who can speak to your academic strengths, character, and leadership potential. Provide them with ample time to write strong, personalized recommendations.
  • Show Impact Through Extracurriculars: Focus on quality over quantity in your extracurricular activities. Highlight your leadership roles, commitment, and the positive impact you've made in your community.
  • Consider Applying Early Decision: Early Decision applicants at Vanderbilt tend to have a higher acceptance rate. If Vanderbilt is your top choice, applying ED could improve your chances of admission.

Vanderbilt's admissions process seeks to identify students who will thrive in its rigorous academic environment and contribute to its dynamic campus culture.
