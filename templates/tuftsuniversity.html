Tufts University Financial Aid Principles

At Tufts, we believe that cost should never be a barrier to a world-class education. That’s why we are committed to meeting 100% of the demonstrated financial need for all admitted undergraduates, regardless of citizenship status. Our financial aid packages are designed to ensure that students can focus on their studies without the worry of tuition costs.

Tufts University follows the principle that education should be accessible to all qualified students, and we make the following commitments regarding financial aid:

  • Admission: Tufts meets 100% of demonstrated financial need for all admitted undergraduate students, regardless of citizenship status. We are committed to providing a pathway for exceptional students from all backgrounds to join the Tufts community.
  • Financial Aid Packages: Tufts offers a combination of grants, student loans, and work-study funding, ensuring that financial aid recipients can focus on their studies and not on how to pay for their education.
  • No Loans for Low-Income Families: Students from families with annual incomes under $60,000 typically receive financial aid packages with no student loans, relying on grant aid and work-study funding to meet their needs.

Tufts University Financial Aid Principles for International Students

At Tufts, we are committed to making education accessible to all qualified students, including international students. While financial aid for international students is limited compared to U.S. citizens, we do offer need-based scholarships and merit-based awards.

For international students, Tufts offers the following principles regarding financial aid:

  • International students are considered for need-based financial aid on a limited basis, with decisions based on academic achievement and financial need.
  • Tufts offers merit-based scholarships to exceptional international students, recognizing achievements in academics, leadership, and extracurricular activities.
  • Financial aid packages for international students may include a combination of need-based aid and merit-based scholarships to reduce the burden of tuition costs.

Tufts University Signature Scholarships

Tufts also offers competitive merit-based scholarships to recognize exceptional academic and extracurricular accomplishments:

  • National Merit Scholarship: Awarded to students who are National Merit finalists, offering $500 per semester for up to eight semesters of enrollment at Tufts.
  • ROTC Scholarships: Students participating in Army, Navy, or Air Force ROTC can use scholarships at Tufts, including full-tuition and partial scholarships.

Experience Tufts

Experience Tufts is a student-led initiative providing funding for students on need-based financial aid, enabling participation in extracurricular activities such as club sports, arts, service trips, and more.

What Tufts Looks for in Applicants

Tufts University seeks students who are intellectually playful, globally minded, civically engaged, collaborative, and kind. The admissions process is holistic, focusing on a blend of academic achievement, extracurricular engagement, personal growth, and a demonstrated passion for interdisciplinary learning. Tufts values students who thrive in collaborative environments and contribute meaningfully to their communities.

Key Qualities Tufts Looks For

  • 📚Academic Achievement: Tufts values a strong academic record, including high grades in challenging courses such as AP, IB, or dual-enrollment classes. While they do not report average GPAs, most admitted students are in the top 10% of their graduating class.
  • 💬Standardized Test Scores: Tufts is test-optional but will consider SAT or ACT scores if submitted. The middle 50% ranges for the Class of 2027 are 1460-1540 for the SAT and 33-35 for the ACT.
  • 🎓Extracurricular Involvement: Tufts values students with meaningful extracurricular achievements, particularly in Tier 1 or Tier 2 activities that show leadership, passion, and impact.
  • 📝Personal Essays: Essays are crucial for showcasing your character, intellectual curiosity, and passion for Tufts. Applicants should articulate their specific interest in Tufts and how they embody its values.
  • 🌍Recommendation Letters: Letters from teachers and counselors provide context about your academic strengths, character, and potential. Choose recommenders who know you well and can provide detailed insights.

Testing and Academic Standards

While Tufts is test-optional, submitting strong scores can bolster your application. The middle 50% test score ranges for the Class of 2027 were:

  • SAT Composite: 1460-1540
  • ACT Composite: 33-35

Students who perform well on standardized tests are encouraged to submit their scores, but applicants without scores will not be at a disadvantage as Tufts reviews applications holistically.

What Sets Tufts Apart

Tufts seeks students who are adaptable, creative, and eager to engage in interdisciplinary learning. The university emphasizes intellectual curiosity, collaboration, and civic engagement. With three undergraduate schools—the School of Arts and Sciences, the School of Engineering, and the School of the Museum of Fine Arts—Tufts provides a dynamic environment for students to explore diverse interests and make meaningful contributions.

Tips for Tufts Applicants

  • Take Challenging Courses: Build a rigorous academic schedule with a focus on core subjects. Engage in AP, IB, or dual-enrollment courses if available, and prioritize intellectual growth.
  • Write Thoughtful Essays: Use your essays to highlight your unique voice, interests, and how you align with Tufts’ values. Demonstrate your intellectual curiosity and passion for interdisciplinary learning.
  • Choose Recommenders Wisely: Select recommenders who know you well and can provide specific examples of your academic and personal strengths.
  • Focus on Impactful Extracurriculars: Highlight activities that demonstrate leadership, passion, and sustained commitment. Depth of involvement is more significant than the number of activities.
  • Visit and Engage with Tufts: Attend campus visits, virtual tours, or Jumbo Chats to learn more about Tufts. Demonstrating interest through these opportunities can strengthen your application.

Tufts’ admissions process is designed to identify students who will thrive in its intellectually vibrant, collaborative, and globally minded community. By excelling academically, engaging deeply in extracurriculars, and expressing your authentic self in your application, you can position yourself as a strong candidate for admission.

Tufts Supplemental Prompts for the Class of 2029:

Applicants to the School of Arts & Sciences or the School of Engineering

Please respond to one of the following three prompts in 200-250 words:

  • It’s cool to love learning. What excites your intellectual curiosity and why?
  • How have the environments or experiences of your upbringing – your family, home, neighborhood, or community – shaped the person you are today?
  • Using a specific example or two, tell us about a way that you contributed to building a collaborative and/or inclusive community.

Applicants to the School of the Museum of Fine Arts (SMFA) at Tufts

Please respond to the following prompt in 200-250 words:

Art has the power to disrupt our preconceptions, shape public discourse, and imagine new ways of being in the world. What are the ideas you’d like to explore in your work?

All Applicants

In addition, we will ask all applicants to complete this sentence in 100 words or less:

“I am applying to Tufts because…”
