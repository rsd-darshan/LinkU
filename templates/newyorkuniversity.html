Financial Aid and Scholarship Regulations

All financial aid is subject to renewal requirements, including maintaining satisfactory academic progress. Eligibility and amounts may vary based on financial need, academic performance, and family circumstances. Be sure to file the FAFSA and CSS Profile by the deadline to be considered for all available aid.

What NYU Looks for in Applicants

  • 📚Academic Performance: NYU values strong academic records, including a competitive GPA and challenging coursework such as AP or IB classes.
  • 📝Standardized Test Scores (Optional): While NYU is test-optional, high SAT or ACT scores can enhance an application if submitted.
  • 🎭Extracurricular Activities: Leadership roles, community service, and a passion for extracurriculars show NYU that you're a well-rounded applicant.
  • 💬Personal Statement and Essays: NYU values thoughtful and authentic essays that reflect your personality, motivations, and life experiences.
  • 📜Letters of Recommendation: Strong recommendations from teachers or counselors who know your abilities and character can make your application more compelling.
  • 🌎Diversity and Unique Experiences: NYU seeks students from diverse backgrounds, perspectives, and experiences to enrich its community.
  • 🗽Global Mindset and Cultural Awareness: NYU values students who are curious about different cultures and global perspectives, reflecting its commitment to a diverse and inclusive campus.
  • 🤝Social Justice Interests: An interest in social justice, whether related to race, LGBTQ+ issues, economic inequality, or other areas, aligns well with NYU’s values.
  • 🔍Fit with NYU: Showing an understanding of NYU’s values, culture, and programs and explaining why NYU is the right place for you is essential.

Suggestions to Get Into NYU

  • 📖Authenticity: Be genuine in your applications. NYU appreciates applicants who are honest about their achievements, aspirations, and interests.
  • 🌎Develop a Global Perspective: Show an interest in cultures, languages, and global issues. NYU values students who are curious about the world and open to different perspectives.
  • 📈Pursue Academic Excellence in Your Areas of Interest: Take challenging courses, such as AP or IB, to show your commitment to academic growth and intellectual curiosity.
  • 📆Engage in Extracurricular Activities Related to Your Passion: Participate in activities that reflect your interests and demonstrate a well-rounded personality. Leadership roles in these areas are a bonus.
  • 💼Use Your Talents to Benefit the Community: NYU appreciates students who contribute to their communities. Volunteer work, social justice initiatives, or community projects aligned with your interests can strengthen your application.
  • 🌍Consider Starting a Project with a Global or Social Impact: NYU values innovation and social responsibility. Starting a business, nonprofit, or project that tackles an issue of importance shows initiative and dedication.
  • 📝Show Growth and Self-Reflection in Your Essays: Thoughtful essays that reflect personal growth, insights, and authenticity are crucial. NYU wants to see your journey and your goals.
  • 🏀Participate in Sports or Hobbies: If you’re passionate about a sport or hobby, pursue it! Commitment to personal interests demonstrates discipline and balance.
  • 🎓Maintain Strong Academic Performance: NYU looks for applicants with a strong academic background. Keep up a good GPA, and if you choose to submit test scores, aim for competitive results.
  • ✍️Write Essays that Highlight Your Potential and Character: Show NYU who you are beyond grades and test scores. Emphasize your goals, values, and what you would bring to NYU’s community.
  • 🌟Demonstrate How You Will Contribute to NYU's Community: NYU values students who show potential to make an impact. Highlight your goals, experiences, and future contributions to the world and the NYU community.

NYU's Supplemental Essay Prompt

Bridge Building at NYU

“In a world where disconnection seems to often prevail, we are looking for students who embody the qualities of bridge builders—students who can connect people, groups, and ideas to span divides, foster understanding, and promote collaboration within a dynamic, interconnected, and vibrant global academic community. We are eager to understand how your experiences have prepared you to build the bridges of the future. Please consider one or more of the following questions in your essay:

  • What personal experiences or challenges have shaped you as a bridge builder?
  • How have you been a bridge builder in your school, community, or personal life?
  • What specific actions have you taken to build bridges between diverse groups, ideas, or cultures?
  • How do you envision being a bridge builder during your time at our university and beyond?

At NYU, we love a good bridge—whether it's one of the iconic physical bridges connecting our global cities or one of the many metaphorical bridges that unite our diverse and international student body. The connections you’ll make here are essential to our community, and we’re excited to learn how your life experiences have prepared you to build them.

The key to writing this essay is understanding that it's an exercise in creative thinking—exactly the type of thinking you will need as a college student and global citizen. Approach it with curiosity and excitement. Consider the big and small ways you have connected people and ideas, both in your school or community and beyond.

Start by brainstorming specific examples that show how you’ve connected groups, ideas, or cultures. Maybe you organized an event that brought people together, or perhaps you’ve worked to mediate conflicts between individuals or groups. Once you have your examples, think about how you can tie them to your plans at NYU. There are countless ways to continue building bridges in college, and we want to know how you plan to do so.

Source: NYU Admissions
