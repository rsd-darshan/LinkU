Dartmouth College Financial Aid Principles

When awarding aid, Dartmouth follows these principles:

  • Dartmouth offers zero parent contribution and no loans for families with annual incomes below $125,000, provided their assets are typical for this income range. This ensures that students from low- to middle-income backgrounds can afford a Dartmouth education.
  • Students from these families are expected to contribute to their education through summer or leave-term earnings, part-time work during the school year, and/or personal savings. The full financial need is met without student loans, though loans are available upon request.
  • Families with annual incomes above $125,000 are still eligible for need-based scholarships. Dartmouth reviews each application individually, and families earning above this threshold may still receive scholarship assistance.
  • No expectation for student loans: Students who qualify for need-based financial aid will not be expected to borrow loans as part of their aid package. Aid is provided through employment and scholarships/grants.

Students and parents may choose to borrow loans to cover the calculated family contribution or additional expenses such as health insurance or a computer.

Dartmouth's Supplemental Essay Prompts

Prompt #1: Why Dartmouth?

As you seek admission to Dartmouth's Class of 2029, what aspects of the college's academic program, community, and/or campus environment attract your interest? How is Dartmouth a good fit for you? (100 words or fewer)

Prompt #2: Personal Reflection (Choose One)

Please respond to one of the following prompts in 250 words or fewer:

  1. There is a Quaker saying: Let your life speak. Describe the environment in which you were raised and the impact it has had on the person you are today.
